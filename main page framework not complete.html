Georgia's History and Culture

Georgia is a southeastern state located in the US which is home to diverse landscapes and people. The culture is vibrant and each city within Georgia has its unqiue charm.
Georgia is home to various beaches, mountains, and forests. It is home to fans of southern traditions like farming, horsebackriding, and fishing.
It is also a sanctuary to those who enjoy nightclubs, concerts, and fine dining in various cities.The state has a tumultous history beginning with the violent colonization
of Native American (Also known as American Indian) land by Europeans. This colonization continued for over a century and during that time European colonialists
were also involved in the Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade. This led Georgia to become the central cotton producer in the United States due to the labor
of Africans who were forced to be slaves. Following the Civil War and Emancipation Proclamation of 1863, Georgia also became a trailblazer in the
Civil Rights Movement with many leaders like Malcolm X, Martin Luther King, and Xernona Clayton changing Georgia, and subsequently the United States forever.[1].


Fun Fact: Our state is known for its welcoming residents and delicious peaches.
